The Roman Currency

The Roman coin would, depending on the time period, consists of either gold, bronze, silver and or orchicalcum. It was first introducted in the Republican period & continued well into the Imperial era. Depending on the period, the material of the coin would differ. There have been notable moments in Roman history in regards to inflation issues. This lead to an Emperor called Diolectian that pushed out reforms to combat it. Due to the longevity of the Empire, many different types of coins from different periods remained in circulation & in fact, it is the most dug up artifact by modern day archaeologists. The vast majority of these coins tend to be from the Imperial period and have contributed heavily to our understanding of the Imperial period. While one might think a simple coin does not provide much value to historical research, it has actually allowed us to document Emperors that have not been documented in ancient text.
