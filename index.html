The Romans

The Roman Empire, previously the Roman Republic, started off as a little unknown village within the Tiber valley. Despite being surrounded by dangerous neighbours, such as the Etruscans, Greeks & Samnites, these people would go on to form one of the largest and most famous Empire's known to humanity Today, little remains of the Roman Empire on a political scale, while we have a plethora of their cultural heritage. Different examples of this include architecture such as aquaducts and monuments, to our modern day languages having roots in Latin, down to the simple Roman Denarii, the Roman coin.

On our website you will be able to find and browse the different antiques that we currently have on display. This month's display include architecture of several buildings, statues for those that prefer the arts, and lastly coinage from the different periods, representing the different Emperors of their age.
